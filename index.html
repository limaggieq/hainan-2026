The Destination

China's tropical
best-kept secret

Hainan Island is China's southernmost province - think tropical beaches, warm water, and lush rainforest with a distinctly Southeast Asian feel. Historically a major hub for Chinese migration to Southeast Asia, the island has a cultural blend that feels unlike anywhere else in China.

It's less crowded than typical mainland China destinations. The Li and Miao ethnic minorities have lived here for thousands of years - their languages, traditions, and crafts are living culture, not museum exhibits. Bill's family has Hmong heritage. The Miao and Hmong are the same broad family, separated by centuries of migration - you can see it in the silver headdresses and the textile patterns.

December is peak season for a reason: dry, sunny, 26°C water. The island runs north to south - Haikou in the north, Sanya on the southern tip. We cover both.

Food highlight: Hainan is the birthplace of Hainanese chicken - poached, served with ginger-scallion oil and rice cooked in chicken broth. You'll find it everywhere. Fresh seafood, coconut-forward dishes, and Southeast Asian influence round out the table.

The Trip

Three segments.
Join any or all.

The trip runs north to south. Each segment stands on its own - pick the one that works for you, or do the whole thing.

Dec 18 (Fri) - Dec 21 (Mon)

Haikou

Westin Haikou · 3 nights

Haikou is the capital of Hainan - a working city, not a resort town. Maggie lived here from age 8–10 and last visited in 2009. This segment is equal parts personal roots and authentic local experience.

  • Traditional tea house breakfast (lao ba cha) - think dim sum but simpler, slower, and more local. A pot of tea, steamed dumplings, coconut milk cakes.
  • Qilou Old Street - 1920s colonial-era arcade shophouses, unchanged since Maggie lived here. Southeast Asian in feel.
  • Roots visit - Maggie's elementary school, the villa where she grew up, and the iconic coconut drink factory around the corner.
  • Hainan Volcanic Cluster Geopark - walk through a 2km lava tube carved 10,000 years ago. Genuinely spectacular geology.
  • Neighborhood night market - real food vendors, not tourist traps. Grilled skewers, fresh fruit, loud and authentic.
🏙️ The roots segment - most personal
Dec 21 (Mon) - Dec 23 (Wed)

Atlantis Sanya

Atlantis Sanya, Imperial Club · 2 nights · Haitang Bay

Atlantis is one of Asia's largest resort-aquarium hybrids - same concept as Atlantis the Palm (Dubai) or Atlantis Paradise Island (Bahamas), but Sanya's own version. The property is themed as an ancient sunken city. 22,000+ marine animals in 12,000 cubic meters of water. Glass tunnels with sharks overhead. A full water park. Private beach. We're in the Imperial Club.

  • The Lost Chambers aquarium - glass tunnels, sharks, rays, jellyfish, seahorses, interactive touch pools. Multiple visits across 2 days.
  • Water park with slides and pools
  • Haitang Bay beach - 26°C, calm and clear
  • Imperial Club lounge: breakfast, snacks, evening drinks
🐠 The aquarium segment - Jordan will not want to leave
Dec 23 (Wed) - Dec 30 (Wed)

Sanya

Park Hyatt Sanya Sunny Bay Resort · 7 nights · Sunny Bay

This is the centerpiece - seven nights over Christmas on Sanya's best beach, with a rotation of cultural day trips and full resort days. Christmas Eve and Christmas Day are anchored here.

  • Christmas Eve: Tropical Paradise Forest Park - tram through the rainforest canopy, orchid valley boardwalk, suspension bridge 40m above the forest floor - then hotel Christmas Eve prix-fixe dinner
  • Christmas Day: Wuzhizhou Island - snorkeling with a 1-on-1 English instructor, Coral Defense Camp (plant a real coral cutting, earn a conservation certificate). Lunch on the island.
  • Nanshan Temple - the 108-meter Guanyin goddess statue standing in the South China Sea
  • Binglanggu Li & Miao Cultural Village - bamboo pole dancing, longhouse village, traditional weaving. Connects to Bill's family heritage.
  • Sunset catamaran on Dec 29 - private charter, 2.5 hrs on the South China Sea
  • Live seafood dinner: pick your fish from the tank

How to Join

You're welcome to join for any part of the trip. The three segments are independent enough to mix and match.

Easiest · Recommended

Hainan Visa-Free

Citizens of 59 countries - including the US, Canada, UK, Australia, New Zealand, Japan, South Korea, and most EU nations - can enter Hainan without a visa for up to 30 days. No advance application. Just bring:

  • Return flight ticket
  • Hotel booking confirmation
  • Travel itinerary within Hainan

A Temporary Entry Permit is issued on arrival at Haikou or Sanya airport. Valid for Hainan Province only. One requirement: you must arrive from outside mainland China. Flying via Hong Kong (our routing) qualifies. If you connect through Shanghai or Beijing, you'll need a standard China visa instead.

Alternative

China Tourist Visa

If you prefer a longer-term option or want to travel elsewhere in China before or after, apply for a 10-year multiple-entry tourist visa through a Chinese Consulate.

  • Allow 4–6 weeks to process
  • Apply as soon as you decide you're coming
  • Valid for mainland China + Hainan
  • Useful for future China travel
